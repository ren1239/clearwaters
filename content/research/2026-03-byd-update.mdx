## Investment Thesis

The bull case for BYD has not changed. The near-term picture has gotten harder, and that is the point.

BYD Company (1211.HK) enters 2026 under visible pressure: domestic market share fell from 25.4% in December to 15.8% in January, a price war with no end date is eroding margins, and a compliance pledge to reduce supplier payment terms to 60 days carries an estimated USD 8–10 billion one-time cash flow hit. On reported earnings, the stock trades at roughly 11x a trough earnings year. Add back the R&D expense that BYD conservatively expenses at 94% rather than capitalising at the peer average of 30%, and the normalised multiple drops to approximately 7.8x — below Toyota, the world's most profitable automaker, which carries none of BYD's battery technology, energy storage, or vertical integration.

The thesis is asymmetric. The bear case, on our conservative model, implies a price of HK$97 — essentially where the stock trades today. The base case, at 10x normalised 2026 earnings, implies HK$121. The stress case, where margins stay compressed and revenue misses by 10%, is HK$88. The downside is narrow. The upside depends only on survival through a difficult two years, not on a recovery.

**Price target: HK$121 (12-month base case). Rating: BUY.**

## Why Now

Three things have converged in early 2026 that make this a more interesting entry than November 2025.

First, the stock has not moved despite BYD surpassing Tesla in global battery electric vehicle volumes — 2.26 million units to Tesla's 1.64 million in 2025. That milestone received no re-rating. Second, the January and February domestic sales data, which triggered a wave of negative coverage, contain a significant seasonal distortion: a longer-than-usual Lunar New Year holiday, the rollback of EV purchase tax exemptions at year-end, and a high comparison base from pulled-forward Q4 2025 sales. The structural headwinds are real, but the market has likely overcorrected on a seasonal trough. Third, the normalised P/E on our conservative estimates now puts the stock below Toyota on a like-for-like basis — a company with no battery IP, no energy storage business, and no vertical supply chain integration. That mispricing is more acute today than it was four months ago.

## Business Overview

### Growth Driver 1 — The International Revenue Ramp

BYD exported 1.04 million vehicles in 2025 — 29% of total group revenue — at an average selling price approximately 40% above its China blended ASP. The international business is not yet a margin story; it is a volume story transitioning toward a margin story as local factories replace exports.

Hungary's Szeged facility began trial production in January 2026 and is targeting mass production in Q2. At full capacity of 200,000 vehicles per year, it eliminates the European Commission's tariff on Chinese-imported EVs and moves BYD toward the 70% local content threshold required for European subsidy qualification. Brazil's Camaçari plant — built on a former Ford complex — began assembling semi-knocked-down units in July 2025 and is targeting 50% local content by end of 2026. Indonesia is completing a 150,000-unit facility for the Southeast Asian market.

We model international auto revenue at RMB 200 billion in 2026, growing to RMB 238 billion in 2027. These are deliberately conservative against BYD's own guidance of 1.3 million international units. Turkey has been quietly cancelled. Brazil carries execution risk following a labour controversy that resulted in a USD 7.5 million civil settlement. The international thesis is intact but requires patience — the full margin benefit of local production is a 2027–2028 story.

### Growth Driver 2 — Energy Storage and Flash Charging

The FinDreams subsidiary supplies more than 20% of cells for Tesla's Shanghai Megapack factory — approximately 8 GWh per year, generating an estimated CNY 3.5 billion in annual revenue. BYD holds a 12% global share of energy storage cells and a 32% share of the LFP energy storage market, targeting 35% in 2026. ESS margins are structurally higher than automotive: no dealer network, longer contract durations, and cell-level cost advantages that third-party producers cannot match.

In March 2026, BYD launched its Flash Charging platform — 4,239 stations live at launch, targeting 20,000 by year-end across 18,000 urban locations and 2,000 highway stations. The defining architectural feature is an integrated 200–300 kWh battery buffer at each charging pile, which draws slowly from the grid and discharges at up to 1,500 kW when a vehicle connects. This eliminates the need for grid capacity upgrades and reduces installation costs by approximately 60%. The international rollout is targeted for end-2026, prioritising Hungary, Brazil, and Southeast Asia.

We model ESS revenue at RMB 43 billion in 2026 growing to RMB 103 billion by 2029 — a faster growth rate than automotive and at improving margins.

### Growth Driver 3 — Consolidation Cycle

China had more than 500 EV brands in 2019. Approximately 80 remain in 2025. The consensus forecast is seven by 2030. The consolidation is not yet complete and the ghost brands — unprofitable but still producing — are keeping pricing pressure elevated across all segments. This is the primary near-term headwind.

It is also the primary medium-term tailwind. BYD entered 2026 with RMB 128 billion in cash and is the only major domestic EV producer running positive operating cash flow at current pricing. As the consolidation cycle eliminates undercapitalised competitors, their volume, dealer networks, and fleet contracts flow toward the survivors. BYD's fully amortised midstream LFP manufacturing base — cells, cathodes, and packs built out since 2018 — means it can sustain the trough longer than any competitor.

## Financial Analysis

The financial picture for 2026–2027 is intentionally sobering.

Revenue grows at 7% in 2026 and 6% in 2027 — a sharp deceleration from the 23% reported in 2024. Gross margin compresses to 18.5% in 2026 and 18.0% in 2027 as domestic price pressure and international factory ramp inefficiencies overlap. Net margin falls to 2.4% in 2026 — the trough — recovering only modestly to 2.5% in 2027. R&D investment holds at approximately 6% of revenue through 2027, peaking in absolute terms at RMB 57 billion as BYD continues developing God's Eye ADAS, the DiPilot platform, and in-house chip design.

Reported EPS falls from HKD 12.52 in 2025 to HKD 8.34 in 2026 — a 33% decline driven by the convergence of DPO compliance, margin compression, and elevated capex. The recovery is gradual: HKD 9.10 in 2027, HKD 11.76 in 2028.

The normalised picture is more stable. BYD capitalises only approximately 6% of R&D against a sector average of 30%. Adding back the gap — roughly RMB 13 billion pre-tax, RMB 10 billion after tax — raises normalised net profit from RMB 22 billion to RMB 32 billion in 2026, and normalised EPS from HKD 8.34 to HKD 12.14. The reported earnings trough is a function of conservative accounting, not underlying deterioration.

Free cash flow turns negative in 2026 — approximately RMB 71 billion — as the DPO compliance one-off hits operating cash flow. BYD enters 2026 with RMB 128 billion in cash. Post-compliance, the balance sheet holds approximately RMB 57 billion, sufficient to fund ongoing operations without distress.

## Risks

**ADAS gap — the primary structural risk.** BYD is categorised as a Tier 3 provider in city Navigate on Autopilot, behind Huawei (Tier 1), Xpeng (Tier 1), and Xiaomi (Tier 2). In the CNY 200,000–300,000 segment — the highest-margin category in the domestic market — BYD fell out of the top five brands in 2024 and has not recovered. The Tang L and Han L, launched in April 2025 as BYD's premium ADAS-equipped flagships, were poorly received. The product refresh with new software architecture is delayed until late 2026. Until that lands and benchmarks against competitors, the premium domestic segment remains structurally impaired.

**DPO compliance — a one-time but significant cash event.** BYD's historical supplier payment terms averaged 155 days, extending in some cases to 275 days. The June 2025 industry pledge sets a 60-day maximum. BYD has publicly committed to compliance. Management argues that 70% vertical integration neutralises the hit — BYD both pays and collects within the new framework. Analysts estimate the one-time cash outflow at USD 8–10 billion regardless. The FY2025 annual report, due April 2026, will provide the first quantified disclosure of compliance progress.

## Valuation

BYD on normalised 2026 earnings trades at approximately 7.8x — a discount to Toyota (9x reported), well below Xiaomi (18x), and a fraction of CATL (25–28x). No peer carries BYD's combination of vertical supply chain integration, energy storage exposure, and international manufacturing footprint.

The base case applies 10x to normalised 2026 EPS of HKD 12.14, implying **HK$121 — 27% above the current price.** No recovery is assumed. No multiple expansion is assumed. The only requirement is that BYD survives the trough with its balance sheet intact, which the cash position of RMB 57 billion post-DPO supports.

## What to Watch in the Next 12 Months

Four data points will determine whether the thesis advances or requires reassessment.

**FY2025 annual report (April 2026):** The single most important near-term catalyst. Watch for DPO disclosure — specifically whether compliance has begun and what the cash position implies for the one-time hit. If cash is below RMB 80 billion and DPO has already started compressing, the thesis timeline extends.

**Domestic market share (March–May 2026):** January and February data were distorted by Lunar New Year and subsidy rollback. If market share does not recover toward 20%+ by May, the domestic structural story is worse than modelled and the ADAS risk has moved from watch to exit consideration.

**Hungary production ramp:** Full production targeted for Q2 2026. If delayed beyond Q4, the international margin uplift moves to 2028. The thesis survives but the base case price target shifts from 2026 to 2027 earnings.

**God's Eye ADAS benchmarks (H1 2026):** BYD's DiPilot 600 city NOA is the product the premium domestic thesis rests on. If independent benchmarks place it in Tier 2 by mid-year, the ADAS risk is closing. If it remains Tier 3, reduce the position on any rally above HK$110.

## Conclusion

BYD in March 2026 is a harder story to tell than it was in November 2025. The domestic data is ugly. The margins are under pressure. The ADAS gap is confirmed and wider than hoped. The DPO cash hit is coming.

It is also, on the numbers, cheaper. Normalised earnings are closer to HKD 12 than the reported HKD 8. The balance sheet is intact. The international factories are live. The ESS business is growing at 55% annually into a market where BYD has structural cost advantages no competitor can replicate within a five-year horizon. The consolidation cycle is running in BYD's favour at every level except the premium domestic segment — and that is a product cycle problem with a known resolution date.

Munger's original insight was not about BYD the automaker. It was about Wang Chuanfu's engineering culture — the willingness to invest through cycles without cutting corners. That culture is visible in the R&D line, which has not been reduced despite the earnings pressure. It is visible in the Flash Charging buffer architecture, which solves the grid capacity problem competitors have been avoiding. It is visible in the decision to build factories in three continents rather than export forever.

The trough is the entry. The thesis is intact.
